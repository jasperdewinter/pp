title: "Modeling the business and financial cycle in a multivariate structural time series model"

We present a novel approach to simultaneously decompose the short-term and medium-term dynamics from a set of macro-economic and financial time-series by means of a combination of their estimated cycles. Our analysis covers the G7 countries and the Netherlands. The main outcomes can be summarized in five points: (1) The cyclical movements of credit volumes and house prices are largely driven by the medium-term cycle; (2) For most countries, the co-movement between the cycles of the financial and macroeconomic variables is mostly present in the medium-term; (3) For all countries considered, we find strong concordance between the medium-term cycles of  house prices and gross domestic product (GDP); (4) The relation between the GDP and credit cycles is more complex. In only three countries we find strong concordance. However, in three other countries we find ‘indirect’ correlation, i.e. the medium term credit and house price cycle share co-cyclicality, which in turn shares commonality with GDP; (5) The cross-country concordance of both the short-term cycle and the medium-term cycles of GDP, house prices and credit is low. Hence, the bulk of the cyclical movements seem to be deiven by domestic rather than global factors.
